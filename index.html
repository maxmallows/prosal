01/04/19-07/04/19


April Fools


Week overview

Monday - Central - Collectors preview for work by Makoto Kagoshima at Messum’s on Cork Street.

Tuesday - Central - At the ICA, curator tour [3-3:30] of current show from Morag Keil. Openings across St James’ and Mayfair, with highlights being Harlem based artist Stanley Squirewell at Gallery 8, Beverley Fishman at Ronchini and large group show at Belmacz. Also talk [6:30-8:30] at Dellasposa on current Future is Female group show. South - Morning press view and evening private view for the Jerwood/FVU Awards at Jerwood Space.

Wednesday - Central - Great set of openings including Gillian Ayres at Alan Cristea, excellent printmaker Tom Hammick at Flowers and Brian Maguire at Alice Black. East - Joan Cornellà at Public Gallery in Stoke Newington also looks interesting.

Thursday - West - Four shows in Holland Park/Notting Hill, with Sanlé Sory at David Hill in Ladbroke Grove being a highlight. Central - Running down from Bond Street, with openings at Parafin, Blain Southern, Lazinc and David Gill in St James’. Also talk [6:30-7:30] from Joan Snyder at Blain Southern coinciding with the opening. East- Walking from Farringdon to Brick Lane starting at Modern Art, to Standpoint, Jealous East and Kate Macgarry, amongst others. Inspiration Series continues at the Sarabande Foundation with talk [7-8:30] from national treasure Martin Parr. SouthEast - Screening [7-9] on doc on the Chicago Imagists at Goldsmiths; the current Imagist exhibition there is well worth a visit.

Friday - Late at Tate Britain with various happenings on social documentary in response to Don McCullin’s current retrospective exhibition. East - Emma Fineman opens at Beers Contemporary by Old Street. SouthEast - Revolv Collective open at Seen Fifteen in the Bussey Building.

Saturday - Central - Morning book signing with Oliver Jeffers at Lazinc, and afternoon opening for Bea Schlingelhoff at Arcadia Missa. East - Head to Herald Street for openings at Maureen Paley, Soft Opening and Project Native Informant.
