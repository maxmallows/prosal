Week overview

Monday - Central - Openings for Gina Pane (1936-90) at Kamel Mennour and also for photographer Haris Nukem at Noho Studios; later will be quite fun. Conceptualist Charles Gaines delivers a lecture on his career and practice at CSM. North - Thomas Langley opens at COB in Camden.

Tuesday - Central - In Mayfair, three openings 5 minutes walk from each other at Chris Beetles, Herrick and also group show at Max Hetzler of artists utilisng digital technology in their painting practice. Art historian Martin Kemp and Larry Keith of the National Gallery deliver joint talk [7-8:30] at the Royal Institution on technical analysis required in establishing the authenticity of works, with particular focus on the Da Vince Salvator Mundi case. North - Opening of works on paper at the Estorick from Milan’s Ramo Collection, including Boccioni, Chirico & Pascali. South East - Sarah Morris & Zhou Li open at White Cube Bermondsey.

Wednesday - Central - New work from Lily Dujourie at Richard Saltoun and a feature length CGI movie by Lawrence Lek, Aidol, goes on show at Sadie Coles delving into the inevitable future of AI influence on music production and the perpetual reality of fading fame. East - Walk from Jakob Brugge at GAO> in Stepney to interim show by Camberwell BA Photography students at Four Corners in Bethnal Green. South East - Spanish artist Marco Godoy opens at Copperfield in Southwark.

Thursday - West - Johnnie Cooper exhibits 50 paintings and sculptures for opening at Saatchi; be sure to rsvp. East - Dealer Liss Llewellyn exhibits German/British post-impressionist Karl Hagedorn (1889-1969) at the Mercers Company in the city; this follows exhibition at Pallant House, Chichester. At the Whitechapel Charles Gaines takes part in panel [7-8] discussing the seminal The Theatre of Refusal exhibition [UC Irvine 93]. South East - Opening for Alan Michael at 3236RLS in Catford.

Saturday - South East - Opening for Alan Michael at 3236RLS in Catford.
