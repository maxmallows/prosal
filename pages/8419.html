All of London's Art Events in One Place

08/04/19-14/04/19


Week overview

Monday - South East - Annie Hall screening at the Dulwich Picture Gallery. .

Tuesday - Central - Gerhard Richter opens at Gagosian, Davies Street, with Sean Scully talk [6:30-7:30] at Blain Southern coinciding with his new exhibition at the National Gallery, and also talk [6:30-8] John Stezaker at The Photographers Gallery. Also London Curatorial and Aindrea Contemporary have a group show opening at Regent’s Place, that will be good.

Wednesday - West - Filmmaker Hito Steyerl opens at The Serpentine. Central - Two painting openings for Chantal Joffe at Victoria Miro and Chloe Wise at Almine Rech. Walking from Bloomsbury to Clerkenwell openings at October Gallery displaying work from its 40 year history, Portugese artist Bruno Pacheco at Hollybush Gardens and then again Chantal Joffe and NS Harsha at Victoria Miro’s main space. Maybe a bike needed.

Thursday - West - Opening for Mohamed Melehi and work from the Casablanca Art School at Mosaic Rooms. Central - Too much. St James’ to Mayfair, mongolian artist Baatarzorig Batjargall at Jack Bell [Mason’s Yard, next to W Cube], then up to Dover St with openings for Giovanni Ozzola at Gazelli and the great William Eggleston at D Zwirner, and Mazzoleni on Albemarle St. Rose English is also in conversation at Richard Saltoun. Oh and Rembrandt’s Self Portrait with Two Circles goes on show at Gagosian. Okay. Soho to Fitzrovia, Derek Jarmanat at Amanda Wilkinson, then neat set of openings at Pilar Corrias and Pi Artworks on Eastcastle St and Rosenfeld Porcini round the corner on Newman St. East - Standouts are Anna-Sophie Berger at Cell Project Space and Mandy El'Sayegh at Chisenhale. South East - Bea Bonafini opens at Bosse & Baum in Peckham. Epic.

Friday - West - West - Opening at Elephant West in White City for their new Asian Moving Image show, and the Serpentine General Ecology Project are holding an evening of events on the theme of nature and eroticism at the Institut Francais in South Ken. Central - Panel discussing the Elizabeth I Armada Portrait at the National Portrait Gallery [also gallery open till 9]. East - Curator tour for Is this Tomorrow at the Whitechapel, and openings at Piper Keys and Kunstraum.

Saturday - West - Panel at Elephant West for aforementioned show. Central - William Eggleston will be signing books [2-4] at David Zwirner, rsvp. East - An interesting looking group show at Auto Italia in Bethnal Green.

Sunday - South East - Afternoon opening for sculptor Brian Catling at Matt’s Gallery in Bermondsey.
